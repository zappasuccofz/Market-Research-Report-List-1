<p><p>The dog urinary catheter market is primarily divided into two types: indwelling catheters and intermittent catheters. Indwelling catheters are designed for prolonged use, remaining in place for extended periods, which helps manage urine output in dogs with chronic conditions or post-surgery. Intermittent catheters are used temporarily, allowing for periodic drainage of urine and are typically chosen for short-term needs, such as during diagnostic procedures or acute health issues. Both types address the specific urinary needs of dogs effectively.</p></p>
