<p><p>The Ultra Clear Low Iron Glass market features two primary types: Floating Glass and Calendered Glass. Floating Glass is produced by floating molten glass on molten tin, resulting in a smooth, flawless finish that enhances clarity and transmittance. Calendered Glass, on the other hand, is processed through rollers that produce a thinner, more flexible product, often used in applications requiring a precise thickness and surface finish. Both types cater to various architectural and industrial applications, emphasizing superior transparency and aesthetics.</p></p>
