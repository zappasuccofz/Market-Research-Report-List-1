<p><p>The biochip market is primarily divided into two segments: microarrays and microfluidics. Microarrays are platforms that allow for the simultaneous analysis of thousands of biological samples, commonly used in genomics, proteomics, and biomarker discovery. Microfluidics, on the other hand, involves the manipulation of small volumes of liquids in confined spaces, enabling high-throughput screening and precise control over biochemical reactions. Both technologies enhance research efficiency, facilitate targeted drug development, and advance personalized medicine applications.</p></p>
